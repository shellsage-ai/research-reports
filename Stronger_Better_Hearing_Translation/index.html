Γ¥î Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  1. Using volume instead of clarity
    What goes wrong: Ears get fatigued, speech still sounds muddy.
    Why: Loud does not equal clear.
    Fix: Reduce noise first (ANC/transparency), then adjust EQ for voice.

  2. Skipping the training log
    What goes wrong: No objective proof of progress.
    Why: Memory is inaccurate under fatigue.
    Fix: Log every session in C:\AugmentLab\strength_log.csv.

  3. Adding weight too aggressively
    What goes wrong: Form collapses, joint pain risk rises.
    Why: Ego progression beats technical progression.
    Fix: Use the +2.5/+5/+10 lb rule only after clean reps.

  4. Treating exosuit as a strength replacement
    What goes wrong: You rely on gear without building capacity.
    Why: Assistance feels easier immediately.
    Fix: Keep 3 weekly training sessions even with exosuit use.

  5. Poor mic/phone placement during translation
    What goes wrong: Wrong captions and weird translations.
    Why: Apps hear noise, not speech.
    Fix: Keep phone 1-2 feet from speaker, avoid loud airflow/fans.

  6. No backup translation app
    What goes wrong: One app fails mid-conversation.
    Why: Language support and network conditions vary.
    Fix: Keep both Google Translate and Microsoft Translator installed.

  7. Ignoring safe listening limits
    What goes wrong: Long-term hearing damage risk increases.
    Why: ΓÇ£Just this onceΓÇ¥ high volume becomes routine.
    Fix: Track dBA and session length; lower volume early.

≡ƒôû Glossary

  • Augmentation: Improving human ability using tools, training, or technology.
  • Active Noise Cancellation (ANC): Earbud feature that reduces background noise electronically.
  • Transparency Mode: Earbud mode that lets outside sound in for awareness.
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio: How strong the voice is compared with background noise.
  • Live Transcribe: Android feature that converts speech to real-time text captions.
  • Machine Translation: Software that converts speech/text from one language to another.
  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increasing training difficulty to drive strength gains.
  • RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion): How hard a set feels on a 1-10 scale.
  • Creatine Monohydrate: Supplement that helps recycle energy for high-intensity effort.
  • Exosuit: Wearable assistive gear that reduces physical strain during movement.
  • dBA (A-weighted decibel): Loudness measure adjusted to human hearing sensitivity.
  • In reserve (reps in reserve): Reps you could still do before failure.
